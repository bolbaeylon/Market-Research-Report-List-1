<p><p>Modular gripper systems are essential in automation, enabling robots to handle various objects efficiently. Parallel gripper systems utilize two parallel jaws that move in unison, ideal for gripping flat or symmetrical items. Angular gripper systems, on the other hand, feature jaws that open and close at an angle, allowing for better access to objects in tight spaces or for irregular shapes. Both types enhance flexibility and precision, catering to diverse industrial applications in robotics and automation.</p></p>
